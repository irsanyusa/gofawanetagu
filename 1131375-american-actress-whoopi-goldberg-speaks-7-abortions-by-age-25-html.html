    Caryn Elaine Johnson, better known by her stage name Whoopi Goldberg, is an American actress, comedian, author, and television host.

    She has been nominated for several awards such as Emmys, Grammys, Oscars, and Tonys.

    Goldberg became the second black woman in history to win an Oscar for acting.

    The actress played Celie on ‘The Color Purple’ and Oda Mae Brown for the film ‘Ghost,’ both roles earned her high accolades.

    Goldberg was the daughter of a nurse and got pregnant at the age of 14 and performed a self-abortion.
