Equatorial Guinea: The Spanish-speaking country in Africa

06 Jul 2024, 00:00

Equatorial Guinea is the only official Spanish-speaking country in Africa. The country was a Spanish colony until 1968, when it gained independence under the “decolonization of Africa” process. Equatorial Guinea was not alone in being under Spanish rule in Africa, though, and Spanish-speaking communities persist in other places on the continent — most notably in Morocco and Algeria.  If you’re looking to visit an African country where Spanish is the official language, Equatorial Guinea (or Guinea Ecuatorial, as it’s known in Spanish) is the place to go.
